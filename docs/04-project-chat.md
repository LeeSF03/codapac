# 4. Chatting with your squad

This page shows you how to talk to your agent squad so they build what you actually want.

## Where the chat lives

Inside any project, the **project chat** is the panel on the right. Every
message stays saved to that project — close the tab and come back, it'll
all still be there.

## The basics

1. Click inside the message box at the bottom.
2. Type what you want in plain English.
3. Press **Enter** to send. (Hit **Shift + Enter** for a new line.)

Your squad reads the message, figures out who should handle it, and
replies right in the chat. If the request needs to become a task, a
card will appear on the **board** automatically.

## What to say

Codapac is happy with normal, human language. You don't need to
"prompt" it.

**Good messages look like:**

- *"Add a pricing page with three tiers — Free, Pro, and Team. Make it feel
  like a friendly startup."*
- *"The hero text on the homepage feels too long. Tighten it and swap in a
  photo instead of the illustration."*
- *"Something is broken — the Sign up button does nothing on mobile. Please
  fix it."*
- *"Add a dark mode toggle to the top bar."*

**Vague messages that slow things down:**

- *"Make it nicer."* (Nicer how? Which page?)
- *"Fix the site."* (Fix what?)
- *"Do the thing we talked about."* (Your squad can only see this chat.)

## Tips for great results

- **Say what you want, not how to build it.** Your squad handles the how.
- **Be concrete about the feeling.** *"Feels like a friendly startup"* or
  *"clean and editorial, like a newspaper"* gives your squad useful direction.
- **One thing per message (mostly).** Big, bundled asks are fine, but
  small, focused asks get reviewed and shipped faster.
- **Point at what you mean.** *"the hero on the homepage"* or *"the
  second pricing card"* is way clearer than *"the top thing."*
- **Examples help.** *"Like Stripe's homepage"* or *"like the attached
  screenshot"* is totally fine.

## Who answers

Your squad is made of three agents. They'll chime in based on what you asked:

- **BOSS** — plans the work, breaks big asks into small tasks, asks
  follow-up questions if your request is ambiguous.
- **FIXER** — actually makes the change and reports back when it's ready
  to review.
- **TESTEES** — checks the result, flags problems, and gives the thumbs up
  when it's working.

You don't need to pick who to talk to. Just say what you want, and the
right agent answers. Meet them properly in [Your agent squad](./07-agents.md).

## Following up

- To push back on a result: *"I don't love the spacing on the pricing
  cards — can you tighten it and make the prices bigger?"*
- To approve something: *"Looks good, ship it."*
- To change direction: *"Actually, drop the Team tier for now."*

Your squad treats each message as the latest version of the truth, so you
can change your mind freely.

## Starting a fresh thread

For now, each project has **one** ongoing project chat — think of it
as the project's memory. If you want to explore a completely different
direction, it's usually cleaner to:

1. Create a **new project** (see [Creating a project](./02-creating-a-project.md)).
2. Keep the original one for the first idea.

## If something goes wrong

- **"My message sent but nothing happened."** Wait a few seconds — your
  squad takes a moment to think. If it's been a minute, try
  *"Are you stuck? Let me know what you need."*
- **"The squad asked a question I don't understand."** Just ask them to
  rephrase: *"Can you say that in simpler terms?"*
- **"The board didn't get any new cards."** Some messages are just
  conversation (e.g. *"Looks good!"*). If you expected tasks, say so:
  *"Please break this into tasks on the board."*
